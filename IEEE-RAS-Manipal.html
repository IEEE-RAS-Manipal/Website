about Robotics & Automation

Humanity has been working towards creating autonomously operating machines ever since our mastery in mechanical engineering. This effort got a major push in the previous century with the advent and the democratization of electronic technologies.

Over the past decade, the field of Robotics has outgrown its initial niche and has established itself as a fully-fledged multi-disciplinary field of engineering itself!

Robotics focuses on systems incorporating sensors and actuators that operate autonomously or semi-autonomously in cooperation with humans. Robotics research emphasizes intelligence and adaptability to cope with unstructured environments.

Automation research emphasizes efficiency, productivity, quality, and reliability, focusing on systems that operate autonomously, often in structured environments over extended periods, and on the explicit structuring of such environments.

ABOUT IEEE RAS

The IEEE Robotics and Automation Society's objectives are scientific, literary and educational in character. The Society strives for the advancement of the theory and practice of robotics and automation engineering and science and of the allied arts and sciences, and for the maintenance of high professional standards among its members, all in consonance with the Constitution and Bylaws of the IEEE and with special attention to such aims within the Field of Interest of the Society.

The Society provides aid in promoting close cooperation and exchange of technical information among its Members and Affiliates, and to this end holds meetings for the presentation of papers an​d their discussion, sponsors appropriate periodicals and special technical publications, and through its committees studies and provides for the needs of its members and affiliates.

ABOUT US

At the Manipal Chapter of IEEE Robotics & Automation Society, we strive to push the global IEEE RAS agenda: foster the development and facilitate the exchange of scientific and technological knowledge in Robotics and Automation domains of engineering to benefit our constituent members, the profession, and humanity.

We aim to do so through activites conducted for constituent members along with seminars and workshops for members and other students alike. Inducted committee members will be given the opportunity to also be part of the behind the scenes working of the organization. They can be part of any two of five subsystems of the organization
